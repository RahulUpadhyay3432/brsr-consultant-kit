## 1. What people are discussing (sentiment)

Genuine community venting lives in closed WhatsApp/consultant channels, not the open web. The
published mood is **bimodal**, and the gap between the poles is Saaksh's positioning lane:

- **"Overwhelmed / manual scramble"** (preparers + MSMEs) — independently corroborated: an
  **NSE + CFA Institute + CFA Society India** study (300 cos, ~70% of market cap, Oct 2024)
  found "giant strides" but persistent **inconsistencies in units + data-source definitions**;
  ~**40% of ESG audit time is spent cleaning data**; **983 listed companies have no assurance
  provider on record**. An IIMA study (1,012 cos) found uneven disclosure.
- **"Box-ticking / hollow"** (academic/legal) — high filing rates, weak data, **no legal
  consequence for poor ESG performance** ("disclose harm and you still comply").
- **"Strategic / maturing"** (vendor sales narrative) — FY2025-26 framed as "the year to start
  building, not just reporting."
- **Fastest-rising, most vivid pain: value-chain / MSME supplier data.** "If you're a small
  spinning mill supplying Page Industries or Raymond, they'll send auditors… can't provide the
  data → you risk losing the contract." Suppliers track EHS "as a side responsibility,"
  "compliance relies on memory," "no evidence chains."

## 2. Regulatory & document landscape (dates, circulars, formats)

**Recent regulatory changes (~last 12 months):**
- **SEBI circular 28 Mar 2025** ("ease of doing business", SEBI/HO/CFD/CFD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2025/42):
  "assurance" → **"assessment or assurance"** (SEBI: "assurance" caused "unwarranted association
  with a particular profession" — deliberately not locking to CAs); **value chain deferred to
  FY2025-26 (voluntary), assessment/assurance FY2026-27**; scope = partners ≥2% of
  purchases/sales, **capped at 75%**; new **Green Credits** leadership indicator (P6).
- **ISF Industry Standards on Reporting of BRSR Core** (SEBI circular 20 Dec 2024) —
  ASSOCHAM+CII+FICCI standardised how to compute BRSR Core metrics.
- **BRSR Core reasonable-assurance glide path**: top 150 (FY23-24) → 250 → **500 (FY25-26)** →
  **1000 (FY26-27)**. ⚠️ **Verify start year before quoting** — some 2025 restatements drop the
  top-150/FY23-24 phase.
- **ESG Rating Providers (ERPs)**: SEBI Master Circular 16 May 2024 (India = first country to
  regulate ERPs; CRISIL/ICRA/CareEdge/SES registered); clarificatory circular 29 Apr 2025;
  **Feb 2026 SEBI working group to review the ERP framework**.
- **IFRS S1/S2 (ISSB)**: India has **not** adopted; BRSR ~40% aligned with IFRS S2; consensus is
  SEBI/MCA/ICAI fold ISSB components into BRSR rather than adopt directly. No date — **watch item**.
- **Regulatory fragmentation**: four regulators (SEBI / MCA / MoEFCC / RBI), distinct timelines.

**Documents / formats people reference** (a "kinds of documents" map):
BRSR format (Section A/B/C, Reg 34(2)(f) LODR) · **BRSR Core** (assured 9-attribute subset) ·
**ICAI Background Material on BRSR, Revised 2024** · **ICSI Compendium on BRSR (4 Aug 2025)** +
ICSI Institute of Social Auditors · ISF Industry Standards · **NSE/BSE filing FAQs** · filed in
**PDF + XBRL** via NEAPS same day as the annual report. **No verified "BRSR-Lite" format exists.**

**Assurance standards**: ISAE 3000 (Revised) today → ICAI **SSAE 3000 / SAE 3410** (ICAI:
"world's first ESG assurance standard") → **ISSA 5000** (IAASB, effective periods on/after
15 Dec 2026). BRSR Core wants source docs per attribute + calculation workbooks + methodology +
**named data owners + evidence chain**.

## 3. Competitive landscape — features Saaksh lacks

**Structural finding: of ~25 tools surveyed, every competitor is enterprise, quote-gated, no
free tier, targeting the corporate filer or investor. NONE targets the independent consultant.**
Pricing is opaque field-wide (only Salesforce ~$210k/yr + $60k Scope-3, Speeki assurance
$75–125k/yr, Novisto ~CA$40k/yr start are public). Saaksh's free / on-device / cited /
consultant-priced wedge is genuinely unserved.

**Biggest competitive threats:**
- **Newtral / "Noa"** (Bengaluru, ~$600k seed, India-first, BRSR-native): agentic multi-agent
  drafting, **direct XBRL→SEBI portal filing**, spend-weighted supplier tiering, ERP sync,
  7-language supplier portal, "audit-ready in ~18 days."
- **Oren**: ships nearly Saaksh's *entire* Pro stack (doc auto-fill + AI narrative + supplier
  Scope 3 + audit trail + multi-framework BRSR) at enterprise scale.
- Defensible lane vs both: **price (free entry) + independent-consultant workflow + on-device
  privacy + cited/no-fabrication**, not feature breadth.

**Recurring feature gaps (ranked by fit for a lean consultant tool):**
1. **AI anomaly / sanity validation** on submitted numbers (Persefoni, Sweep, Novisto, IBM,
   Breathe, Credibl, ESG Pulse). Cheap, high-trust, fits no-fabrication. Clearest near-term win.
2. **Prior-year value inline at the owner's input** + **stale-data flags** (Novisto, Watershed).
3. **One-click framework-formatted export, incl. Word** (Diligent, Breathe, Novisto, Coolset).
4. **Supplier-lite Collect / value-chain Scope 3 collection** (Newtral, Oren, Lythouse, Cority,
   CO2 AI) — rides the FY26-27 mandate.
5. **Maker-checker review + per-disclosure comment threads** (Workiva, Sweep, TSC, Coolset).
6. **AI gap-spotter vs last year's filed report + peers** (Watershed, Workiva).
7. **XBRL export → SEBI filing** (Newtral, TSC, SAMESG, Credibl) — most common *hard* gap.
8. **Conversational/agentic NL query over the client's data** (Credibl "EVA", Salesforce Agentforce).
9. **OCR of scanned bills / meter readings / handwritten docs** (4Seer, SAMESG, ESG Pulse).
10. **Double materiality** (SAMESG, Sweep, Position Green) — Saaksh ships a shortlist by design.
11. **Reduction / "what-if" scenario + SBTi target-setting** (Plan A, Greenly, Sweep, IBM).

## 4. Trust & credibility — table-stakes vs differentiators

**A. Security / data-trust (procurement WILL check):**
- **ISO 27001 is the cert Indian listed-company procurement asks for by name** (Tata, Reliance,
  Infosys, HDFC, Adani empanelment). **SOC 2 is the US standard, largely unknown to Indian
  procurement.** → ISO 27001 first (~₹4–12L, 3–6 mo), SOC 2 later only for US/global.
- **Missing table-stakes today**: privacy policy (retention + third-party-sharing +
  deletion-rights + last-updated date), Terms, DPA, **public sub-processor list**, Trust/Security
  page, status page. A 20-tool scan found **100% had a buyer-visible trust gap in <60s**.
- Encryption in transit + at rest, annual pen test — assumed baseline.

**B. DPDP Act 2023** — Saaksh is a Data Fiduciary, almost certainly NOT an SDF:
- Hard enforcement **13 May 2027**; Data Protection Board **live since Nov 2025**; buyers demand
  readiness sooner. Biggest exposure: **Mixpanel 100% session-replay + autocapture + GA4 firing
  before consent** — needs granular, unbundled, pre-consent gating.
- Cross-border currently **permitted** (negative-list, no restricted list issued) — US-hosted
  infra legal for now; **India data-residency (Supabase ap-south Mumbai) is a commercial
  sales-unblocker**, not a current legal must.
- Also needed: plain-language notice, named **grievance officer**, Rule-6 security basics.

**C. Methodology transparency — Saaksh already WINS (surface it):**
- Per-factor provenance (source + version year + region + uncertainty + unit + scope) is the
  Climatiq-grade standard. Saaksh already does this + cited-to-SEBI/ICAI + no-fabrication. Most
  ESG vendors can't claim it. → a public **"How we calculate / Methodology"** page is a free
  credibility multiplier. (Aspirational strongest signal: independent engine review, TÜV/ISO
  14064-3 style — most vendors can't concretely claim it either.)

**D. Social proof:**
- Minimum: **named + attributed testimonials** (Priya — real, photographed, titled), customer
  logos ("Trusted by"), ≥1 **industry-specific case study** (~5× generic), founder/advisor bios,
  third-party reviews (**Gartner Peer Insights** near-term; **Verdantix Green Quadrant** medium;
  Magic-Quadrant/Forrester not near-term).
- **India-specific**: registered legal entity + **GSTIN displayed** (footer/Terms) is itself a
  signal CFOs verify on the GST portal.
- **Anti-patterns**: stock-photo/anonymous testimonials, "trusted by thousands" with no names,
  only-5-star reviews, no SSL, no status page, undated privacy policy, misconfigured DMARC/SPF
  (request emails land in spam / are spoofable — relevant to Collect).

## 5. Government / senior-leader commentary

- **SEBI** (28 Mar 2025 circular rationale): changed "assurance"→"assessment or assurance"
  because "assurance" caused "unwarranted association with a particular profession."
- **CA Charanjot Singh Nanda, ICAI President**: ICAI's **SSAE 3000** is "the first such assurance
  standard in the world"; CAs as "trusted advisors who enable businesses to thrive in a green
  economy," "bridging the gap between compliance requirements and business performance."
- **Shubhashis Dey** (SEBI ESG Advisory Committee member): "garment/automobile firms are largely
  assemblers, the actual operations occur at the supply chain level… the regulator must ensure
  the entire value chain adheres to the same ESG standards."
- **Rajesh Patel** (CEO Snowkap): "the supply chain here is vast, made up of small businesses
  that don't have the resources to track ESG data… India's ESG journey is early but moving fast."

## 6. Where Saaksh is genuinely differentiated (the moat)

1. **Free, on-device, no-login BRSR gap analysis cited line-by-line to SEBI/ICAI** — no
   competitor offers a privacy-first, client-side, free readiness tool.
2. **Consultant is the user** — proposal/fee builder, chase-the-client's-internal-teams Collect,
   who-owns-what map, per-field explainers, CBAM/CCTS readiness. No analog in the ~25 tools (all
   target the corporate CSO or the investor).
3. **Cited / no-fabrication AI** + per-factor provenance — a trust angle vs broad generative claims.
4. **Affordability for the long tail** the enterprise platforms price out.
