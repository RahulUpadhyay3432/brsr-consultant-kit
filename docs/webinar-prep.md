# Saaksh — Walkthrough Prep (Priya's training · 30-min solo slot)

You're presenting **solo, ~30 minutes, live**: walk through the tool and answer
whatever the room asks. Audience = practising ESG consultants. The hard part isn't
the features (you know those) — it's saying them **in a consultant's language**: their
pain first, then how Saaksh removes it. This doc gives you exactly that, line by line.

**How to use this:** read §3 (the demo script) and §5 (the Q&A) out loud twice until
they're in your own words. That's where the confidence comes from. Everything here is
accurate to the tool as it is **today** (June 2026).

---

## 0. The one mindset that makes this easy
You are not selling software. You are a person who **felt the BRSR scramble** and built
the tool you wished you had. Consultants trust that. So lead every feature with *their*
pain, not the feature. The format is always:

> "You know how **[painful thing they do every engagement]**? Watch this." → click → "So
> that just **[removed the pain]**."

If you do nothing else, do that.

---

## 1. Setup & safety (do this BEFORE the call)
- **Pre-load a sample client.** Fill the intake form beforehand for a vivid example — a
  **steel manufacturer, listed top-1000, first-time filing, exports to the EU, with a
  couple of filings ticked (PAT, Hazardous Waste)**. Steel + EU makes the Beyond-BRSR
  (CBAM/CCTS) demo light up. Have `/report` already generated in one tab, and the filled
  form in a second tab, so you never type live.
- **Backups.** Screenshot every screen you'll show (or a 60-sec screen recording). If the
  live site hiccups, switch to images and never lose the room.
- **Be a presenter.** Message Priya the day before: *"Could you set me up as a co-host /
  presenter so the screen-share runs smoothly?"* — not "am I allowed to share?".
- **Time plan:** ~2 min why → ~16 min demo → ~12 min Q&A. It's interactive, so let
  questions come during the demo; the Q&A buffer is your cushion.
- **Have the deflections ready** (pricing, data privacy, AI) — §5. Those are the three
  that always come up.

## 2. Open (≈2 min) — the why, in their words
Say something close to this, unscripted:

> "Quick show of hands — when you start a new BRSR engagement, how does week one
> actually go? For most people it's the same scramble: a blank 108-field format, the
> client's data sitting in five different places, and no quick way to see what's already
> covered in last year's filing. You spend the first week just figuring out *where you
> are*. That's the problem I kept hitting, so I built Saaksh. It's a compliance-reporting
> workspace that **starts with BRSR**. Let me just show you — I'll generate a real
> readiness report live."

Then go straight to the demo. Don't do slides-first; the live tool is your weapon.

## 3. The demo script (consultant-lens, click by click)
Go in this order. For each beat: **what you click → their pain → what you say → the aha.**
Keep moving; you're showing breadth, not exhausting each screen.

### Beat 1 — Generate the report  *(tab with the filled form → "Generate BRSR Readiness Report")*
- **Their pain:** the blank 108-field format and the week-one "where do I even start".
- **Say:** "I've filled a two-minute intake — sector, listing, what's in their last filing.
  Watch what happens when I hit generate."
- **Aha:** the report appears instantly. "That blank format is now a gap-analysed plan. No
  account, no upload — it ran **on my device**."

### Beat 2 — Overview  *(lands on the Overview tab)*
- **Their pain:** no quick read on how ready the client actually is.
- **Say:** "Top of the report — a readiness score. It's telling me what share of the
  applicable BRSR fields are already covered by their existing filings versus what we
  still have to collect." (The gauge counts up — let it.)
- **Aha:** "In one screen I know exactly where this engagement stands."

### Beat 3 — Action Plan  *(click "Action Plan" tab)*
- **Their pain:** going field by field through 108 disclosures, deciding what's done.
- **Say:** "Every disclosure is sorted into three buckets — **Ready** (already in a
  filing), **Verify** (partly there), and **Collect** (nothing yet). I can filter by
  principle, by status, search."
- **Expand a P6 row** → **Say:** "Open any field and there's a plain-English explanation —
  *what it's asking* and where the data lives. Written from the public SEBI definition, so
  even a junior on your team gets it. And the SEBI source is cited right here."
- **Show the calculator on a P6 row** → **Say:** "And where it needs maths — Scope 1 & 2,
  energy, water, even **Scope 3** — the calculator is built in, with the CEA and IPCC
  factor and its version printed on every line. No hunting for factors, no spreadsheet."
- **Point at "Upload last year's report"** → **Say:** "I can upload last year's report and
  it flags, on my device, which disclosures already appear in it."
- **Aha:** "So the gap analysis that takes you a week is done, and it's cited."

### Beat 4 — Alignment  *(click "Alignment" tab → "Export (CSV)")*
- **Their pain:** the same client data demanded by GRI, TCFD, IFRS, TNFD — re-mapping by
  hand, every time.
- **Say:** "Every BRSR disclosure is mapped to its equivalent in GRI, TCFD, IFRS and TNFD,
  plus the MSCI and DJSI ratings. **Collect once, report many.** And I can export the whole
  crosswalk as a spreadsheet." (Click Export.)
- **Aha:** "The cross-framework translation you redo every engagement — that's a click."

### Beat 5 — Beyond BRSR  *(click "Beyond BRSR" tab)*
- **Their pain:** CBAM and CCTS landing, and not knowing which clients are in scope.
- **Say:** "Because this client is a steel exporter to the EU, the tool flags **CBAM —
  likely applies** and **CCTS — likely applies**, each with the timeline, a readiness
  checklist, and cited sources. It's a readiness check — who's in scope and what to
  prepare."
- **Aha:** "You can walk into the client already ahead of the next regulation."

### Beat 6 — Templates  *(click "Templates")*
- **Their pain:** hunting at 11pm for a materiality format or a way to answer Principle 5.
- **Say:** "The formats you always end up rebuilding — a BRSR response workbook, a
  materiality grid, a stakeholder plan — downloadable here, plus short cited how-to guides
  for the hard bits like human rights and assurance."
- **Aha:** "The stuff you keep in a messy folder, in one place, cited."

> **Everything so far is FREE and runs on the consultant's device.** Say that out loud
> before you switch to Pro — it's the whole trust story.

### Beat 7 — Pro / Collect  *(go to the Collect side — `/requests`, or describe it / use screenshots)*
- **Their pain (the #1 time-sink):** chasing numbers that live with different people — the
  energy manager, HR, the plant — over email and WhatsApp, for weeks.
- **Say:** "When you move from *preparing* to *actually doing the work*, that's Pro. This is
  **Collect**. I add the people who hold each number, and Saaksh emails them a branded
  request with their fields. They submit through a **no-login** form, attach the bill or
  invoice as evidence, and it auto-reminds them so you're not chasing."
- **Aha:** "Chase the data without chasing people."

### Beat 8 — Pro / the AI importer  *(on a campaign — describe or show)*
- **Their pain:** re-keying numbers out of last year's PDF by hand — spreadsheet archaeology.
- **Say:** "Instead of copying numbers out of their old report, I upload it and the AI
  **pre-fills the figures** — and shows me the exact source sentence each one came from,
  for me to verify. It never invents a number; I tick what's right."
- **Aha:** "Hours of copy-paste, gone — and still defensible because you verified each one."

### Beat 9 — Pro / assurance + the narrative draft
- **Their pain (rising fast in 2026):** assurers no longer just ask "is the number right?"
  — they ask "show me the evidence trail and **who** the data owner is for every KPI."
- **Say:** "Because every figure came in through Collect, Saaksh builds an
  **assurance-readiness ledger** — each number, who submitted it, the evidence document,
  and the cited factor behind any calculation. One export for the assurer. And it can draft
  the **qualitative narrative** from the collected numbers — review-ready prose that never
  invents a figure."
- **Aha:** "When BRSR Core assurance comes knocking, you're ready — and it drafted the
  write-up for you."

### Beat 10 — Pro / proposal & fee builder
- **Their pain:** scoping and *pricing* an engagement — "what do I even quote?"
- **Say:** "And before any of that — winning the work. You put in the scope and your own
  rates, and it builds a client-ready proposal with a transparent fee breakdown. It never
  invents a market price; it structures *your* numbers."
- **Aha:** "It helps you win and price the work, not just deliver it."

### Close the demo (15 sec)
> "So: free, it prepares the report — gap analysis, calculators, frameworks, all cited, on
> your device. Pro is the workspace that does the actual work — collecting, the AI, the
> assurance trail, the drafting, and winning the engagement. It starts with BRSR and grows
> to CBAM, CCTS and broader ESG."

## 5. Anticipated questions (say these out loud, honestly)

**"How much is Pro / what does it cost?"**
> "Pro's in early access, priced **per engagement** — not a year-round fee for seasonal
> work. Tell me about the client you're working on and we'll figure out what's fair; I'm
> onboarding the first few manually right now."
(Do **not** quote a number. The honest reason: you're still discovering the right price
with the first customers.)

**"Does my client's data leave my browser? Is it safe?"**
> "The free readiness tool is **100% on your device** — nothing is uploaded or stored. The
> plain-English explanations are pre-written and static, so nothing about your client is
> sent anywhere. Collect, the paid side, deliberately *does* store data — securely, in a
> locked database with private evidence storage — because the whole point is gathering it.
> And the two AI features (reading an old report, drafting the narrative) send the **text**
> to a grounded model to do their job; the document itself is read in your browser. I'm
> upfront about that line."

**"How accurate are the numbers / where do the factors come from?"**
> "Every factor is cited with its version on the line — CEA's grid factor, IPCC 2006 for
> fuels, DEFRA 2024 for Scope 3. Scope 3 is honestly framed as a screening estimate, since
> BRSR treats it as voluntary. Nothing is invented, and a certified GHG auditor should
> still sign off on the final figures — the tool gets you there faster and defensibly."

**"Can I trust the AI? Does it make things up?"**
> "AI here only ever **explains, reads, or drafts — it never decides or invents.** When it
> pulls a number from a document it shows you the exact source sentence, and nothing is
> applied until *you* tick it. The narrative draft writes a gap as '[to be completed]'
> rather than guess. That's deliberate — what you put in front of a client has to be
> defensible."

**"Can it do GRI / TCFD / IFRS / CBAM — the frameworks I actually use?"**
> "BRSR maps to GRI, TCFD, IFRS and TNFD today, exportable as a crosswalk. CBAM and CCTS
> are in as readiness checks now; deeper support lands as the platform grows. The idea is
> collect once and report across all of them."

**"Is it only BRSR?"**
> "It **starts** with BRSR because that's the most urgent and the most built-out. But it's
> positioned as a compliance-reporting workspace — CBAM, CCTS and broader ESG are where it
> goes next. Same evidence-first approach, more regulations over time."

**"Is this only for independent consultants? We're an in-house team."**
> "The work is the same whether you're a consultant or in-house — gap analysis, chasing
> data, computing, reporting. It's built for whoever does that work. Plenty of value either
> way."

**"We could just build this ourselves / use ChatGPT."**
> "For one disclosure, sure. But this is the whole chain — collecting from the client's
> team, computing with cited factors, building the evidence trail, mapping across
> frameworks, drafting — integrated and defensible. That's not a weekend script, and it
> frees you to keep the advisory layer, which is where your value is."

**"How do I get Pro / get started?"**
> "The free tool you can use today, no login. For Pro, there's a 'Request access' button —
> I onboard the first few personally so I can set you up right and learn what you need."

**"What if it's wrong / who's responsible?"**
> "You are the professional in the loop — everything is reviewable and cited, and nothing
> is auto-filed. The tool makes you faster and gives you the sources to defend every line;
> it doesn't replace your judgement."

## 6. Honesty guardrails (protect your credibility)
- **Live today:** the whole free tool; Collect; the AI importer; the assurance ledger; the
  AI narrative draft; cross-framework export; the proposal & fee builder.
- **Coming (say "coming", don't imply it's here):** multi-client workspace, the consultant
  network.
- **Don't overclaim CBAM/CCTS:** they're **readiness checks** (who's in scope, what to
  prepare) — *not* liability calculators yet. Say that plainly.
- **Never quote a Pro price.** Per engagement, early access, "let's talk."
- **The privacy line is precise** (see the Q&A) — free = on-device; Collect = secure
  backend; the AI features send text to the model. Being exact here builds trust; fudging
  it loses the room.

## 8. If you wobble
- **Don't know an answer?** "Great question — let me get you the exact detail right after
  this." Write it down. Never bluff; the room is expert and will catch it.
- **Demo hiccups?** Switch to your screenshots and keep talking. The story is the point,
  not the live pixels.
- **Going long?** Drop Beats 6 and 10 first (Templates, Proposal) — keep Generate →
  Overview → Action Plan → Alignment → Beyond-BRSR → Collect.
- **Keep it practical, not salesy.** It's Priya's training — you're showing a useful tool
  to peers, not closing a sale. That posture is what earns the trust (and the leads).
- **Lean on the demo.** When in doubt, click and let them watch. Saaksh sells itself in
  motion better than any sentence.
