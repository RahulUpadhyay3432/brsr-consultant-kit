# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Independent ESG consultants in India who prepare BRSR (Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting) reports for their clients. They work solo or in small firms. They are experienced compliance professionals — not developers — who understand regulatory filings but need to move fast. They use this tool at the start of a new client engagement, often in front of the client, to quickly diagnose what data already exists and what needs to be gathered. Time is billable; the tool must feel instant and credible.

## Product Purpose

A free web tool that takes a structured 7-field intake form and generates three ready-to-use outputs in under one second: a BRSR data collection checklist with gap analysis (showing which fields are already covered by the client's existing compliance filings), a pre-populated materiality assessment for the client's industry, and a cross-framework mapping table (BRSR to GRI, TCFD, IFRS S1/S2). No login, no data storage, no backend — entirely client-side. Success is a consultant who uses this on a Monday morning client call and leaves with a data collection plan the client immediately understands.

## Brand Personality

Precise · Professional · Calm

The voice is a knowledgeable senior colleague, not a salesperson. Every label, tooltip, and explainer should read as if written by someone who has filed 20 BRSR reports and knows exactly what matters. Specific, not aphoristic. Practical, not inspiring.

## Anti-references

- **Flashy SaaS marketing sites**: No purple gradients, no hero animations, no "Transform your ESG journey" copy. No Webflow-template feel. No gamification. No celebration confetti on form submit.
- **Government portal aesthetic**: Dense unstyled tables, no visual hierarchy, Times New Roman, MCA-filing feel.
- **Cluttered data dashboards**: 12 simultaneous filters, sidebar within sidebar, information overload. Every screen should have one primary task.

## Design Principles

1. **Credibility through restraint.** The consultant is showing this to a client. If the tool looks like a startup demo, the consultant loses face. Every visual choice should reinforce expertise, not enthusiasm.
2. **The data is the product.** Decoration competes with the data. Hierarchy, spacing, and color exist to make 108 disclosure fields scannable — not to make the page beautiful for its own sake.
3. **One task per screen.** The intake form collects context. The report view presents findings. Never mix them. Within the report, one tab is one job.
4. **Plain language over regulatory language.** BRSR is full of dense statutory phrasing. The tool's job is to translate, not to quote. Labels, hints, and gap notes should be written for a consultant who is smart but pressed for time.
5. **Instant perceived performance.** Even if computation is fast, the UI must signal that it worked. Loading states, smooth transitions, and progress indicators matter here because the user often has a client watching.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort WCAG 2.1 AA: good contrast ratios (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text), keyboard-navigable interactive elements, meaningful alt text and aria-labels. No formal audit required. No known screen-reader-first audience, but don't break assistive tech.
